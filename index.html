Starting My Barbershop: A Journey of Skill, Passion, and Purpose

Starting a barbershop wasn’t just a business decision—it was the beginning of a lifelong dream. I’ve always been passionate about grooming, style, and creating spaces where people feel confident and taken care of. But I didn’t want to just cut hair; I wanted to build something deeper: a place that trains, uplifts, and launches careers.

When I started planning the shop, I knew I wanted more than just chairs and mirrors. I envisioned a space where aspiring barbers could come not only to work but to learn. There are so many talented individuals out there with no clear path into the industry, and I saw an opportunity to bridge that gap.

Real Talk: It’s Not Easy

Some days it feels like I'm biting off more than I can chew. There are moments where I question if I'm moving too fast or not fast enough. But the truth is, no one becomes great without pushing through that phase of being unsure.

What keeps me going is the belief that this journey is worth it. I don’t have a perfect shop yet. I don’t have a full roster of clients. But I do have the drive to keep showing up and getting better.

Why I'm Sharing This:


I’m writing all of this down because I want to document the process—not just the wins, but the learning, the awkward starts, and the small victories along the way. If you’re also trying to build something from nothing, maybe this can be a space where we figure things out together.
